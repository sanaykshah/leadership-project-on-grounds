About LPG

The Leadership Project on Grounds is a student-led leadership community at the University of Virginia. Our initiative is to connect undergraduates with alumni, faculty, and leaders across Grounds, so students can learn from real experience, build relationships, and grow as leaders in a supportive, peer-driven environment.

What we do

Dinner Series & intimate programs

Family-style dinners and small-format events with deans, faculty, and alumni, built for real conversation, not just networking buzzwords.

Cross-Grounds connections

We bring together students from different schools, majors, and corners of student life, so your network looks like UVA actually looks.

Student-led, purpose-driven

Programming is shaped by LPG members and exec: peer energy with room for mentorship from folks who’ve walked the path before you.

Leadership isn’t a title: it’s a practice. LPG is a place to practice with people who will push your thinking and have your back.

Dinner Series: Dean Hutson (School of Architecture)

Spring 2026 · Pavilion IX (Dean’s residence), the Lawn · University of Virginia

  • Architecture & built environment
  • Community development
  • Sustainability & justice

A full house for LPG’s first Dinner Series event with Dean Hutson, hosted at the Dean’s residence, Pavilion IX on the Lawn. The evening brought together students from across Grounds around one table for a family-style dinner: thoughtful questions, great food, and meaningful conversation.

Attendees were engaged, curious, and open to learning from one another’s perspectives. The conversation ranged from navigating the world after graduation to favorite buildings on Grounds, sustainability, and even favorite music, while also making space to hear about Dean Hutson’s path as a leader in community development, environmental justice, and urban planning.

It was a strong kickoff for the Dinner Series, with thanks to Dean Hutson for hosting and to everyone who made the night memorable, including LPG’s Dinner Series chair and executive team.

The best part wasn’t any single answer: it was hearing how many different “whys” people brought to the same table.

Opportunities for undergrads

LPG doesn’t exist in a vacuum. These are starting points on Grounds and beyond. Use them to explore, build skills, and find people who care about the same questions you do.

  • Student orgs & leadership roles

    Browse CIOs, apply for exec boards, and find your people through Hoos Involved (AT UVA). The Student Council and CIO fair season are great moments to shop around, even if you already have a home org.

  • Career discovery & advising

    The University Career Center offers coaching, workshops, and industry connections. Pair that with faculty mentors and LPG’s own conversations. You’ll hear multiple lenses on the same next step.

  • Fellowships & high-impact programs

    Keep an eye on programs like the Meriwether Lewis Institute for Citizen Leadership and opportunities through schools like the Batten School. Many welcome applicants from across the University, not just one major.

  • Service & civic engagement

    Madison House anchors student volunteering in Charlottesville; Grounds-wide days of service and CIO-led projects are easy ways to lead through action, not titles.

  • Research & faculty connection

    Office hours are underrated. Whether you’re curious about a syllabus topic or a future capstone, LPG’s faculty-facing events are practice for those one-on-one conversations, then follow up with the professors you click with.

  • Alumni & mentorship

    LPG’s own programming brings alumni to the table; broader networks like the UVA Alumni Association offer programs and regional chapters. Mentorship works best when you show up prepared with curiosity.
